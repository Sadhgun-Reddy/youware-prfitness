1. PRODUCT OVERVIEW
1.1 Product Name
FitSync Pro
1.2 One-Line Elevator Pitch
FitSync Pro is a full-stack gym management and member experience platform that unifies attendance tracking, progress monitoring, personalized training plans, and diet guidance into a single digital ecosystem for gym owners and their members.
1.3 Product Description
FitSync Pro is a web-based application built for independent gym owners and personal trainers who want to digitize and scale their coaching operations without losing the personal touch that defines premium fitness guidance. The platform serves two primary actors — the Admin (the gym owner or head trainer) and Users (registered gym members) — and adapts its feature set intelligently based on the membership tier a user holds.
At its core, FitSync Pro solves three persistent problems in the independent gym space: accountability gaps (members stop attending without consequence), progress invisibility (neither trainer nor client has a structured record of physical change over time), and plan delivery friction (trainers write workout and diet plans on paper, WhatsApp, or generic spreadsheets that are hard to track or version). By consolidating all of these workflows into one authenticated, role-based platform, FitSync Pro replaces scattered tools with a single source of truth for the gym's operations.
For members on the Normal Membership Plan, FitSync Pro provides geo-verified gym check-ins, a mandatory weekly progress submission system, and access to a curated workout video library. For members who upgrade to the Personal Training Membership Plan, the platform unlocks a premium layer that includes trainer-created personalized workout plans, customized nutrition guidance, and direct trainer notes tied to their profile. The Admin panel gives the trainer a comprehensive dashboard to monitor all member activity, review weekly progress submissions, assign and update plans, and manage memberships — all from a single interface. The platform is designed to grow with the business, with a future AI-assisted plan generation module already factored into the architecture roadmap.
 
2. BUSINESS OBJECTIVES
2.1 Primary Business Objective
Provide the gym owner with a branded, self-owned digital platform that increases member retention, improves training outcomes, and creates a recurring revenue stream through tiered membership subscriptions — reducing reliance on informal communication channels and manual tracking methods.
2.2 Secondary Objectives
•	Reduce the administrative burden on the trainer by centralising member data, progress records, and plan management in one platform, saving a minimum of 5 hours per week of manual tracking work.
•	Increase member accountability and gym visit consistency by introducing geo-verified daily check-ins, which create a transparent attendance record visible to both the member and the trainer.
•	Create a meaningful upgrade path from Normal to Personal Training membership by gating premium features behind a higher-value tier, thereby increasing average revenue per user (ARPU) over time.
•	Build a structured, timestamped progress archive for every member that can be used to demonstrate training effectiveness, build trainer credibility, and support future upselling of additional coaching services.
•	Establish the technical foundation for an AI-assisted plan generation system that, in a future phase, will allow the trainer's methodology and expertise to be codified and scaled to serve more members without proportionally increasing the trainer's time investment.
2.3 How This Product Supports the Broader Business Strategy
The gym owner's broader strategy is to run a high-accountability, results-oriented personal training gym that justifies a premium price point in a competitive local market. FitSync Pro directly supports this by transforming the gym's intangible value proposition — expert, personalised guidance — into a visible, trackable digital experience. Members who can see their own progress data are more likely to renew their membership. Trainers who can access historical member data are better positioned to make evidence-based adjustments to plans. Over time, the platform also serves as a data asset: the accumulated progress, attendance, and plan data can inform smarter coaching decisions and, eventually, the AI plan generation system.
2.4 Revenue Model and Monetisation Approach
•	Normal Membership Plan: Monthly or quarterly subscription fee paid online via Razorpay at the time of plan activation. This tier covers attendance tracking, weekly progress submissions, and the workout video library.
•	Personal Training Membership Plan: A higher-priced monthly subscription that includes all Normal Plan features plus personalised workout plans, customised diet plans, and trainer remarks. The price differential between the two tiers represents the platform's upsell lever.
•	Razorpay Integration: All payments are processed through Razorpay's payment gateway, which supports UPI, credit/debit cards, and net banking. Payment status is reflected in real time in the Admin dashboard, gating access to features upon successful payment confirmation.
•	Future Revenue: The planned AI plan generation module could be offered as a premium add-on or used to increase the number of Personal Training members the trainer can serve, effectively increasing revenue capacity without adding staff.
2.5 Expected Business Impact with Measurable Targets
•	Achieve 80% monthly active usage rate among all registered members within 6 months of launch (measured as members who log at least one gym check-in per week).
•	Increase Personal Training Plan conversion rate to 40% of total active members within 12 months of launch.
•	Reduce trainer's administrative time spent on plan delivery and progress follow-up by 60% within 3 months of the Admin panel going live.
•	Achieve a member renewal rate of 70% or higher at each billing cycle within 9 months of launch.
•	Onboard 100% of existing gym members onto the platform within 60 days of the public launch.

8. WIREFRAMES & DESIGN DIRECTION
Visual wireframes will be produced in Figma during the design sprint. The following section provides textual wireframe specifications for every key screen, along with the navigation flow and design direction to guide the UI/UX designer.
8.1 Key Screen Descriptions
Screen 1: Registration – Step 1 of 3 (Personal Info)
Layout: Centered single-column card (max-width 480px) on a gradient background using the platform's primary navy colour. Progress indicator bar at the top of the card shows Step 1 of 3 with the current step highlighted in orange.
Elements (top to bottom): Platform logo + name at top of card. Page title: 'Create Your Account'. Input fields: Full Name, Email Address, Password, Confirm Password, Date of Birth (date picker), Gender (radio buttons: Male / Female / Prefer Not to Say). Primary CTA button: 'Continue to Step 2' (full-width, orange fill). Secondary link: 'Already have an account? Log in'.
Interactive: Inline validation on blur for each field. Password strength indicator below the password field. CTA is disabled until all fields pass validation. On click, data is saved to local state and the component transitions to Step 2.
Responsive: On screens below 480px, the card fills the full viewport width with 16px horizontal padding. The progress bar becomes a stepper with numbered circles.
Screen 2: Member Dashboard (Normal Plan)
Layout: Responsive sidebar navigation (desktop) that collapses to a bottom tab bar (mobile). Sidebar items: Home, Check In, My Progress, Video Library, My Profile. Content area is full-width on mobile.
Home section elements: Greeting card with the member's name and current membership plan badge. Membership status card showing plan name, expiry date, and a Renew button (if expiring within 7 days). Attendance streak card showing current consecutive check-in days and a weekly calendar heatmap. Check In button (large, full-width on mobile) — green when active, grey with 'Already Checked In' when done for the day, and orange-bordered with warning text when outside the 100m radius. This week's progress card — shows submitted status (green tick) or Submission Due prompt (orange, clickable).
Interactive: The Check In button triggers browser geolocation on click. A loading spinner shows while the location is being fetched and verified. Success/failure state replaces the button.
Screen 3: Member Dashboard (Personal Training Plan – Additional Elements)
In addition to all Normal Plan elements, the PT dashboard adds: My Workout Plan card — shows today's exercise count and a View Plan CTA linking to the full plan. My Diet Plan card — shows today's meal count and a View Plan CTA. Trainer Remarks card — shows the most recent trainer remark with a View All link. All three PT cards use a highlighted orange-border style to distinguish them as premium content.
Screen 4: Workout Plan View (PT Member)
Layout: Day-selector tab strip across the top (Mon–Sun). Active day tab is highlighted in primary navy. Below the tab strip, an exercise list for the selected day. Each exercise card shows: Exercise name (linked to the video library entry if one exists), sets, reps, rest period, trainer note (collapsed by default, expandable). At the bottom: Plan last updated date and trainer name.
Screen 5: Weekly Progress Submission Form (Sunday Only)
Layout: Full-page form, single column, with a sticky header showing 'Week of [Sunday Date]'. Form sections: Body Weight (numeric input, kg label, required). Body Measurements (grid of 3 inputs: Chest, Waist, Hips — all in cm, optional). Progress Photos (drag-and-drop upload zone accepting JPEG/PNG, max 5 MB per photo, up to 3 photos). Submit button (full-width, primary orange) at the bottom. On non-Sunday days, the entire form is replaced by a locked state card showing the next submission date.
Screen 6: Admin Dashboard Overview
Layout: Fixed top navigation bar with the platform logo, admin name, and logout. Below the nav bar, a full-width content area. The home view shows 5 KPI cards in a responsive grid (2 columns on tablet, 5 on desktop): Total Active Members, PT Members, Today's Check-ins, Submissions This Week, Expiring Memberships. Below the KPI cards, a 2-column layout: left column shows Today's Check-In Live Feed (scrollable list), right column shows Recent Submissions Pending Review (clickable list items that open the submission detail).
Screen 7: Member Profile (Admin View)
Layout: Profile header with member photo, name, plan badge, join date, and membership status. Below the header, a tab navigation bar: Overview, Attendance, Progress, Workout Plan, Diet Plan, Remarks. Each tab loads the relevant content without a full page reload. Overview tab shows registration details (height, weight, age, goal, health conditions). Workout Plan tab shows the current plan with an Edit Plan / Create New Plan button. Diet Plan tab shows the current meal plan with similar controls.
8.2 Navigation Flow (Text-Based)
Public Route: Landing Page → Registration (3 steps) → Plan Selection → Razorpay Payment → Member Dashboard
Member Flow: Member Dashboard → [Check In | My Progress → Submission Form | Video Library → Category → Video Player | My Workout Plan | My Diet Plan | Trainer Remarks | My Profile → Edit Profile]
Admin Flow: Admin Login → Admin Dashboard → [Member List → Member Profile → [Attendance | Progress Review | Workout Plan Builder | Diet Plan Builder | Remarks] | Video Management → [Upload Video | Edit/Delete] | Submissions Review | Today's Check-ins]
8.3 Design Direction
Visual Style: Clean, modern fitness-tech aesthetic. High contrast between background and action elements. Use of data visualisation (charts, calendars, streaks) to make progress tangible. The UI should feel professional and motivating — not clinical.
Colour System: Primary: Deep Navy #1A3C5E (trust, structure). Accent: Energetic Orange #E87722 (action, motivation). Background: Off-White #F8FAFC. Text: Near-Black #1C1C1C. Success: #1E7A45. Error: #C0392B.
Typography: Headings — Inter or Poppins, bold weights. Body — Inter, regular/medium. Monospace (for measurements/stats) — Roboto Mono.
Reference Products (visual and UX inspiration): (1) Whoop App — for the dark-mode fitness dashboard aesthetic and progress ring visualisations. (2) MyFitnessPal — for the structured dietary logging interface pattern. (3) Notion — for the clean, structured admin and plan management layout approach. (4) Razorpay Dashboard — for the admin KPI card design language. (5) Trainerize — for the workout plan builder UX pattern.
8.4 Recommended Design Tools
Wireframing & Prototyping: Figma (primary). Use Figma components and auto-layout for responsive design. Design tokens (colours, typography, spacing) to be defined in a shared Figma library before screen design begins. Handoff to developers via Figma's Inspect panel or a Zeplin export. All screens must include mobile (375px), tablet (768px), and desktop (1280px) frames.

12. RELEASE STRATEGY
12.1 v1.0 MVP Scope
The MVP will focus on the core loop that delivers immediate value to both the trainer and members: register, pay, check in, track progress, access videos, and manage plans. The following features are in scope for v1.0:
•	F-001: Multi-Step Registration Form
•	F-002: Membership Plan Selection & Razorpay Payment
•	F-003: Role-Based Authentication
•	F-004: Proximity-Gated Gym Check-In
•	F-005: Attendance History Calendar
•	F-006: Admin Today's Check-In Live Feed
•	F-007: Sunday Progress Submission Form
•	F-008: Member Progress Chart & History
•	F-009: Admin Progress Review & Remarks
•	F-010: Categorised Video Library (Member View)
•	F-011: Admin Video Upload & Management
•	F-012: Workout Plan Builder (Admin)
•	F-013: Diet Plan Builder (Admin)
•	F-014: Member Plan View Dashboard (PT Tier)
•	F-016: Admin Overview Dashboard
•	F-017: Member Management Table
12.2 Phase 2 Scope (3-6 Months Post-MVP)
Phase 2 will be informed by real-world usage data and member feedback collected in the first 3 months post-launch. Planned additions include:
•	F-015: Trainer Remarks Feed (deferred from v1.0 if bandwidth constrained)
•	F-018: Membership Renewal Management & Admin Expiry Notifications
•	In-app push notification system (Sunday progress reminders, plan update alerts, membership renewal prompts)
•	Branded email receipts and welcome emails via an SMTP service (e.g., Resend or SendGrid)
•	Admin-configurable gym settings panel (GPS coordinates, check-in radius, progress submission day)
•	Nutrition API integration for the diet plan builder (automatic calorie and macro calculation from a food database)
•	Member referral or invite-a-friend flow
•	Advanced attendance analytics for the admin (weekly/monthly heatmaps, drop-off rate alerts)
12.3 Phase 3 / Future Vision
Phase 3 represents the platform's evolution from a management tool into an intelligent coaching assistant:
•	AI-Assisted Plan Generation: A model trained on the trainer's historical plans and member outcome data generates draft workout and diet plans for new PT members. The trainer reviews and edits the draft before assigning it, acting as a quality gate.
•	Native Mobile Application (iOS and Android): A dedicated mobile app with push notifications, camera integration for easier progress photo uploads, and home screen gym check-in shortcut.
•	Multi-Trainer Support: Allow the gym to add assistant trainers with role-based access to specific member groups.
•	Member-Facing Progress Analytics: Deeper insights including body composition trend analysis, predicted goal achievement date based on current trajectory, and personalised milestone celebrations.
•	Integrated Group Class Scheduling: Session booking, capacity management, and waitlist management for group training classes.
12.4 Feedback Collection Strategy
During the first 30 days post-launch, the trainer will conduct a weekly informal check-in with 5 members (rotating) to gather qualitative feedback on usability and feature completeness. A structured feedback form will be available in the platform's footer for any member to submit suggestions at any time. All feedback will be reviewed by the product team and categorised as Bug, Enhancement, or New Feature. Enhancements and new features will be evaluated against the Phase 2 backlog and prioritised for the next development sprint. Bug reports will be triaged within 24 hours and resolved within 72 hours for critical issues.
